A contractor's ability to perform on a large job is determined almost entirely by what they can put on-site. Renting equipment for every job introduces scheduling risk, availability gaps, and costs that get passed to the client. We've invested heavily in our own fleet so that none of that uncertainty touches your project.

**Air supply** is the backbone of any blast operation. We run:
- Multiple **375 CFM compressors** — deployable simultaneously for multi-pot setups
- A **900 CFM compressor** — the backbone for high-production runs on large structures where volume and pressure consistency are non-negotiable

With that air capacity, we can run **up to four blast setups simultaneously** on a single job site. That's four operators producing in parallel — on separate sections of a structure, on multiple pieces of equipment, or across different elevations at the same time.

**Containment and cleanup:**
- **Dump trailers** for spent media and waste removal

Owning this equipment means it shows up when the job starts — not when a rental yard decides it's available.

## Safety Training and Compliance

Large construction contractors have compliance requirements for every sub they put on a job site. OSHA recordkeeping, safety documentation, site-specific orientations, respirator fit testing, hazard communication — if your project requires it, we're prepared to meet it.

Our crews operate under:
- **OSHA 10 and OSHA 30** training for supervisory and field personnel
- **Respiratory protection programs** with documented fit testing — mandatory for any operation involving abrasive media and airborne particulate
- **Silica (crystalline) exposure controls** in compliance with OSHA's 29 CFR 1926.1153 construction silica standard, including exposure assessments and engineering controls
- **Hazmat awareness** for lead paint and coating removal on older structures, including containment, air monitoring, and waste handling protocols
- Full **insurance and liability documentation** to satisfy GC prequalification requirements

We've been working on commercial and industrial job sites across Michigan since the 1980s. We know what compliance looks like on a union job site, a manufacturing facility, and a municipal infrastructure project. We don't need hand-holding on documentation.
